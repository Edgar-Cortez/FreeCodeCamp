Satoshi Tajiri

Creator of PKMN

  • 1965 - Born in Tokyo, Japan.
  • 1981–1986 - wrote and edited a fanzine called Game Freak focusing on the arcade game scene and met Ken Sugimori.
  • 1989 - Tajiri and Sugimori evolved the magazine into the video game development company Game Freak and makes first game Mendel Palace.
  • 1990 - conceived the idea of PKMN.
  • 1990–1995 - Pitches idea to Nintendo and begins to develop PKMN under the mentorship of Shigeru Miyamoto.
    • During this time, PKMN Red & Blue had to be put on hold several times due to financial problems. Game Freak would create other titles in this time, including:
    • 1991 - Yoshi
    • 1993 - Maio & Wario
    • 1994 - Pulseman
  • The title was a major success and set Game Freak on a course to "worldwide phenomenon".
  • 1999 - Marks the release of the second installment of the series: PKMN Gold & Silver
  • 2002 - Marks the release of the third installment: PKMN Ruby & Sapphire and Satoshi's shift into a more Executive role
  • 2004 - is when the remake of PKMN Red & Green was released.
    • It was the same as the original game with updated graphics and mechanics to be used in future releases.
    • This also marks the start of a pattern we would see for later installments of the series: New Generation then a remake of an older generation.
  • 2006 - Release of PKMN Diamond & Pearl
  • 2010 - Release of the remake PKMN HearGold & SoulSilver
  • 2011 - Release of PKMN Black & White
  • 2012 - Release of PKMN Black & White 2
  • 2013 - Release of PKMN X & Y
  • 2014 - Release of remake PKMN Omega Ruby & Alpha Sapphire
  • 2016 - Release of PKMN Sun & Moon
  • 2017 - Release of PKMN Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon
  • 2018 - Release of PKMN Let's Go, Pikachu! & Let's Go, Eevee!
    • This is a remake of PKMN Yellow
    • First PKMN title to be released on the Nintendo Switch

Satoshi Tajiri

"Places to catch insects are rare because of urbanizations. Kids play inside their homes now, and a lot had forgotten about catching insects. So had I. When I was making games, something clicked and I decided to make a game with that concept."
